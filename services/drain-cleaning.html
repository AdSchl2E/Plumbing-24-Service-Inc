Our drain cleaning specialists address all types of drain issues throughout Fort Myers homes and businesses:

  • Kitchen sink clogs from food waste, grease, and soap scum
  • Bathroom drain blockages caused by hair, soap, and toiletries
  • Toilet clogs from excessive paper or non-flushable items

Our Professional Drain Cleaning Methods

Advanced techniques for effective drain and sewer line cleaning

Hydro Jetting

Our high-pressure water jetting system blasts away years of buildup, grease, sand, and even tree roots from pipes. This eco-friendly method thoroughly scours pipe walls, providing a comprehensive clean.

Drain Snaking

Our professional-grade drain augers and snakes can reach deep into pipes to break up and remove stubborn clogs without damaging your plumbing. Perfect for targeted blockage removal.

Video Camera Inspection

Our specialized waterproof cameras allow us to visually inspect the inside of your pipes, pinpointing the exact location and nature of blockages before and after cleaning.

Bio-Clean Treatment

We offer environmentally-friendly enzyme treatments that digest organic waste in pipes. Great for maintenance and preventing future buildup in your Fort Myers home's plumbing system.

The Right Method for Your Clog

Our technicians will determine the best drain cleaning approach based on the location, severity, and type of clog you're experiencing. For complex or recurring drain issues, we often combine methods for the most effective solution.

Signs You Need Professional Drain Cleaning

Don't ignore these warning signs of drain issues in your Fort Myers home

1
1

Slow Draining Water

If water in your sink, shower, or tub is draining slower than usual, it's often the first sign of a developing clog that needs professional attention before it becomes a complete blockage.

2
2

Recurring Clogs

If you find yourself frequently plunging drains or using chemical drain cleaners, there's likely a deeper issue that needs professional equipment to properly resolve.

3
3

Multiple Clogged Drains

When several drains in your Fort Myers home are backing up simultaneously, it often indicates a main sewer line issue rather than individual drain problems. This requires immediate professional attention.

4
4

Unusual Gurgling Noises

Gurgling sounds from drains, toilets, or pipes when water is draining indicates air trapped in the plumbing system due to partial blockages that will only worsen over time.

5
5

Foul Odors

Unpleasant smells coming from drains often indicate decomposing food, waste, or sewer gas escaping through dried-out P-traps. Our professional cleaning eliminates these odors at their source.

Drain Cleaning FAQ

Common questions about our drain cleaning services

Most standard drain cleaning services take between 30 minutes to 2 hours, depending on the severity and location of the clog. Simple sink or shower drain clogs are typically resolved in under an hour, while main sewer line cleaning may take longer. Our technicians will provide a time estimate after assessing your specific situation.

We don't recommend using store-bought chemical drain cleaners. These harsh chemicals can damage pipes, especially older ones, and they're often ineffective against serious clogs. They can also pose health risks and harm the environment. Our professional drain cleaning methods are both more effective and safer for your plumbing system and the environment.

For residential homes, we recommend having your main sewer line and primary drains professionally cleaned every 1-2 years as preventative maintenance. Homes with multiple residents, older plumbing systems, or lots of tree roots may benefit from more frequent cleaning. Restaurants and commercial properties typically require quarterly or bi-annual drain cleaning service.

Drain snaking uses a flexible auger to physically break through or retrieve clogs, creating a hole through the blockage to restore flow. While effective for simple clogs, it doesn't clean the pipe walls. Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to scour the entire pipe interior, removing all buildup, grease, and debris. Hydro jetting provides a more thorough cleaning and is better for preventing future clogs, though it may not be suitable for very old or damaged pipes.

To prevent future clogs:

  • Use drain strainers in all sinks, showers, and tubs
  • Don't pour grease or oil down kitchen drains
  • Only flush toilet paper and human waste down toilets
  • Run hot water down the drain after each use
  • Pour a pot of boiling water down drains weekly
  • Schedule regular maintenance cleanings

We also offer maintenance plans that include periodic treatments to keep your drains flowing smoothly.
