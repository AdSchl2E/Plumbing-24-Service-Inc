Common Toilet Problems We Fix

Our experienced technicians can diagnose and resolve all types of toilet issues, including:

  • Running toilets that waste water and increase utility bills
  • Leaking toilets at the base, tank, or supply line connections
  • Weak flushing or incomplete waste removal
  • Frequent clogging requiring regular plunging
  • Ghost flushing or phantom refills when no one is using the toilet
  • Noisy operation including whistling, gurgling, or hissing sounds

Whether you're dealing with a minor toilet issue or need a complete replacement, our Fort Myers plumbers have the expertise and equipment to provide the right solution for your needs and budget.

Signs You Need Professional Toilet Repair

Don't ignore these warning signs of toilet problems in your Fort Myers home

1
1

Constant Running

If your toilet continues to run long after flushing, you could be wasting hundreds of gallons of water each day. This is often caused by a faulty flapper, fill valve, or float mechanism that needs professional attention.

2
2

Water Around the Base

Pooling water around the toilet base indicates a leak that could be damaging your floor and subfloor. This often signals a broken wax ring seal or cracked toilet base that requires prompt professional repair.

3
3

Frequent Clogs

If your toilet clogs regularly despite normal use, there may be a partial obstruction in the trap or deeper in your sewer line. Our professional plumbers can diagnose and clear these stubborn blockages properly.

4
4

Weak or Incomplete Flushing

When your toilet doesn't flush completely or requires multiple attempts, it could indicate problems with water flow, clogged rim jets, or issues with the flushing mechanism that require expert repair.

5
5

Age and Inefficiency

If your toilet is over 15-20 years old, you're likely wasting water with every flush. Older models use 3.5-7 gallons per flush, while modern high-efficiency toilets use just 1.28 gallons or less. Upgrading can significantly reduce your water bill.

Toilet Repair FAQ

Common questions about our toilet repair and installation services

Toilet repair costs in Fort Myers typically range from $120-$300 depending on the specific issue. Minor repairs like replacing a flapper or fill valve generally cost less, while more involved repairs like replacing a wax ring or fixing significant leaks may cost more. We always provide upfront, transparent pricing before beginning any work.

A standard toilet installation by our experienced plumbers typically takes 1-2 hours if no complications arise. This includes removing the old toilet, preparing the area, setting the new toilet with proper sealing, connecting the water supply, and testing operation. Complex installations or situations requiring floor repairs may take longer.

We generally recommend repairing rather than replacing if your toilet is less than 10-15 years old and the issue is minor. However, replacement may be more cost-effective if your toilet is older, frequently requires repairs, uses excessive water (more than 1.6 gallons per flush), or has cracks in the porcelain. Our technicians can assess your specific situation and provide honest recommendations.

Modern high-efficiency toilets use as little as 1.28 gallons per flush compared to older models that use 3.5-7 gallons. For a family of four, this can save more than 16,000 gallons of water annually, reducing your water bill significantly. Many Fort Myers residents see a return on their investment within 1-2 years through reduced water bills. Additionally, some water utilities offer rebates for installing water-efficient toilets.

We recommend and install quality toilet brands including Kohler, American Standard, TOTO, and Delta. These manufacturers offer reliable performance, water efficiency, and good warranties. The best choice depends on your specific needs, budget, and bathroom configuration. Our plumbers can help you select the right toilet for your home during our consultation.
