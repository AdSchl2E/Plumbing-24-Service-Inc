Water Heater Types We Service

Explore the various water heating options for your Miami home

Traditional Tank Water Heaters

Conventional water heaters that store and heat water in 30-80 gallon tanks. We install and repair electric, gas, and propane models from all major manufacturers.

Tankless Water Heaters

On-demand water heaters that heat water only when needed, providing endless hot water while using less energy. Perfect for homes with high hot water demand.

Heat Pump Water Heaters

Energy-efficient systems that extract heat from the air to heat water, using significantly less electricity than standard electric units. Ideal for Florida's climate.

Commercial Water Heaters

High-capacity water heating systems designed for businesses, apartment complexes, and other commercial applications with high-volume hot water needs.

Signs You Need Water Heater Service

Warning signs that indicate your water heater needs professional attention

1
1

No Hot Water

If your water heater stops producing hot water completely, this could indicate a failed heating element, thermostat issue, or gas supply problem that requires immediate professional attention.

2
2

Water Leaking Around the Unit

Puddles or moisture around your water heater often indicate a leak in the tank, connections, or valves. This needs immediate repair to prevent water damage and potential flooding in your Miami home.

3
3

Strange Noises

Rumbling, popping, banging, or hissing sounds from your water heater typically signal sediment buildup in the tank or problems with the heating elements. Professional flushing or repair can resolve these issues.

4
4

Rusty or Discolored Water

Brown, rusty, or cloudy hot water often indicates corrosion inside your water heater tank or deteriorating anode rod. This affects water quality and signals your system may be approaching failure.

5
5

Age and Inefficiency

Most water heaters last 8-12 years. If yours is approaching or exceeding this age range, or if you notice increasing energy bills without higher usage, it may be time for a replacement with a more efficient model.

Water Heater FAQ

Common questions about water heater repair and installation

Traditional tank water heaters typically last 8-12 years with proper maintenance, while tankless water heaters can last 15-20 years or more. Factors affecting lifespan include water quality in Miami (hard water can reduce lifespan), usage patterns, maintenance frequency, and the quality of the original installation. Regular flushing and anode rod replacement can significantly extend your water heater's life.

For many Miami homeowners, tankless water heaters are worth the higher initial investment due to their longer lifespan (15-20+ years), energy savings (20-30% more efficient), endless hot water supply, and space savings. They're especially beneficial for larger households with high hot water demand or homes planning to stay in their property long-term. While installation costs more upfront, the lower operating costs and longer service life typically provide a positive return on investment, with some homeowners seeing complete cost recovery within 5-7 years.

In many cases, we can offer same-day water heater replacement for Miami homes. A standard tank water heater replacement typically takes 2-3 hours, while tankless installation may take 4-6 hours if replacing a tank model (due to potential venting and gas line modifications). We keep common water heater models in stock and have relationships with suppliers for quick access to specialized units. For emergency situations, we prioritize restoring your hot water as quickly as possible.

Water heater sizing depends on your household size and usage patterns. For tank systems, we typically recommend:

  • 1-2 people: 30-40 gallons
  • 2-3 people: 40-50 gallons
  • 3-4 people: 50-60 gallons
  • 5+ people: 60-80 gallons

For tankless systems, sizing is based on flow rate (gallons per minute) and temperature rise needed. Our technicians will perform proper calculations based on your specific needs, considering factors like simultaneous hot water usage and Florida's groundwater temperatures.

To maximize your water heater's lifespan and efficiency:

  • Flush the tank annually to remove sediment buildup
  • Check and replace the anode rod every 3-5 years
  • Inspect pressure relief valve operation yearly
  • Check for leaks around connections regularly
  • Set the temperature to 120°F for safety and efficiency
  • Consider installing a water softener if Miami' water is hard in your area

We offer professional maintenance plans that include these services and more, ensuring optimal performance and longer system life.
